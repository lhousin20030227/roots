# DIALOGUE SYSTEM

## Purpose

The Dialogue System delivers story, character development, and investigation naturally through conversation.

Dialogue should always feel believable, concise, and emotionally authentic.

---

# Dialogue Philosophy

People rarely say everything they know.

Players must understand the truth by combining dialogue with evidence and observation.

Dialogue supports the investigation—it never replaces it.

---

# Dialogue Types

## Main Story Dialogue

Required conversations that advance the main narrative.

These conversations unlock:

* New objectives
* New locations
* New evidence
* Story progression

---

## Optional Dialogue

Additional conversations that expand:

* Character backgrounds
* World building
* Family history
* Lore

Optional dialogue never blocks story progression.

---

## Reactive Dialogue

NPC responses change depending on:

* Current chapter
* Collected evidence
* Previous conversations
* Completed objectives
* Relationship with the player

The world remembers what the player has done.

---

## Ambient Dialogue

Background conversations between NPCs.

These conversations:

* Make the world feel alive.
* Reveal small details.
* Add atmosphere.

Most ambient dialogue is optional.

---

# Dialogue Structure

Every conversation follows a logical flow:

```text
Greeting
    ↓
Topic
    ↓
Player Response
    ↓
NPC Response
    ↓
New Information
    ↓
Conversation Ends
```

---

# Player Choices

Dialogue choices influence:

* Conversation flow
* Relationship development
* Available questions

They do **not** create completely different story paths.

ROOTS tells one consistent narrative.

---

# Evidence Integration

Certain dialogue options appear only after discovering specific evidence.

Examples:

* Show a photograph.
* Present a letter.
* Mention an official record.
* Ask about a discovered name.

Evidence unlocks better questions.

---

# Dialogue Rules

Dialogue should be:

* Natural
* Short
* Purposeful
* Emotionally believable

Avoid long monologues.

---

# Voice Acting

Requirements:

* Natural pacing
* Emotional authenticity
* Minimal exaggeration
* Clear pronunciation

Silence and pauses are important parts of conversations.

---

# UI

During dialogue, the player sees:

* NPC Name
* Spoken Text
* Available Responses (if applicable)
* Skip Button
* Replay Last Line

The interface should remain minimal.

---

# Performance Rules

* Conversations load instantly.
* Lip sync matches speech.
* Dialogue history is stored.
* Previously completed conversations are remembered.

---

# Forbidden Features

Do not include:

* Endless dialogue loops
* Unskippable conversations
* Fake choices
* NPCs repeating identical lines forever
* Dialogue that reveals information before the player earns it

---

# Final Rule

Dialogue in ROOTS should feel like speaking with real people.

Every conversation should either deepen a relationship, reveal meaningful information, or move the investigation one step closer to the truth.
