# LIGHTING SYSTEM

## Purpose

The Lighting System defines how light is used to support realism, atmosphere, navigation, and storytelling in ROOTS.

Lighting should guide the player's attention naturally without feeling artificial.

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Lighting is a storytelling tool.

It should communicate:

* Time of day
* Mood
* Safety
* Isolation
* Emotional tone

The world should always feel believable.

---

# Lighting Types

## Natural Lighting

Primary light source during daytime.

Examples:

* Sunlight
* Skylight
* Window light

Natural light changes according to the time of day.

---

## Artificial Lighting

Human-made light sources.

Examples:

* Ceiling lights
* Lamps
* Street lights
* Flashlights
* Computer monitors
* Television screens

Every light source should have a believable origin.

---

## Dynamic Lighting

Certain lights react during gameplay.

Examples:

* Light switches.
* Opening curtains.
* Power outages.
* Flashlight activation.

Transitions should be smooth.

---

# Time-Based Lighting

Lighting changes throughout the day.

Morning:

* Soft sunlight.
* Long shadows.

Afternoon:

* Bright natural light.
* Neutral atmosphere.

Evening:

* Warm indoor lighting.
* Cooler outdoor lighting.

Night:

* Minimal natural light.
* Interior lights become more important.

---

# Story Lighting

Lighting supports emotional moments.

Examples:

* Warm lighting during comforting memories.
* Cold lighting during difficult discoveries.
* Soft sunset during the ending.
* Dim lighting inside abandoned rooms.

Lighting should enhance emotion without becoming unrealistic.

---

# Player Guidance

Lighting may subtly draw attention to:

* Important rooms.
* Interactive objects.
* Open pathways.
* Story-critical locations.

Never use unrealistic glowing objects or exaggerated highlights.

---

# Shadows

Shadows should:

* Be realistic.
* Match light sources.
* Update dynamically when necessary.
* Improve depth perception.

Avoid distracting or flickering shadows.

---

# Indoor vs Outdoor

Indoor lighting depends on:

* Windows
* Lamps
* Room layout
* Weather

Outdoor lighting depends on:

* Time of day
* Weather
* Street lighting

Transitions should feel seamless.

---

# Performance Rules

To optimize performance:

* Use baked lighting where possible.
* Use dynamic lighting only when necessary.
* Limit real-time shadow casting.
* Disable distant light calculations when appropriate.

---

# Design Rules

* No unrealistic colored lighting.
* No flashing lights without narrative purpose.
* No invisible light sources.
* Every room should have believable illumination.
* Darkness should create atmosphere, not frustration.

---

# Final Rule

Lighting in ROOTS should feel invisible to the player.

Its purpose is not to impress—it is to quietly guide attention, reinforce emotion, and make every location feel like a real place that people once lived in.
