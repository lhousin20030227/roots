# AUDIO SYSTEM

## Purpose

The Audio System defines how sound is used in ROOTS to strengthen immersion, storytelling, exploration, and emotional impact.

Audio should communicate information naturally without overwhelming the player.

---

# Audio Philosophy

Sound is part of the storytelling.

Silence is just as important as music.

Players should often hear the world before they see it.

---

# Audio Categories

## Ambient Audio

Background sounds that make each location feel alive.

Examples:

* Wind
* Birds
* Rain
* Distant traffic
* Clock ticking
* House creaks
* Air conditioning
* Electrical hum

Each location has its own ambient profile.

---

## Environmental Audio

Sounds generated by the environment.

Examples:

* Doors opening
* Drawers sliding
* Footsteps
* Light switches
* Water dripping
* Floor creaks
* Windows opening
* Keys turning in locks

Every interactive object should have appropriate sound feedback.

---

## Character Audio

NPC-related sounds.

Examples:

* Footsteps
* Breathing
* Coughing
* Laughing
* Crying
* Speaking
* Movement sounds

Audio should match the character's emotional state.

---

## Music

Music is used sparingly.

It should support emotion rather than constantly play.

Music is appropriate during:

* Chapter openings
* Emotional discoveries
* Important conversations
* Ending scenes
* Credits

Exploration relies mostly on ambient sound.

---

## Voice Audio

Dialogue must sound natural.

Requirements:

* Clear speech
* Appropriate pacing
* Natural pauses
* Emotionally authentic performances

Avoid exaggerated acting.

---

## Evidence Audio

Audio evidence includes:

* Cassette tapes
* Voice recordings
* Voicemail
* Radio broadcasts
* Answering machine messages

Players may replay recordings at any time.

---

# Spatial Audio

All sounds should use 3D positioning where appropriate.

Players should identify sound direction naturally.

Examples:

* Footsteps upstairs.
* Door closing in another room.
* Rain outside a window.
* Radio playing in a nearby room.

---

# Dynamic Audio

The audio system adapts to gameplay.

Examples:

* Rain becomes louder outdoors.
* Indoor ambience changes when windows open.
* Music fades during conversations.
* Ambient sound changes with location.

Transitions should be smooth.

---

# Priority Rules

When many sounds occur simultaneously:

1. Dialogue
2. Critical Story Audio
3. Puzzle Audio
4. Environmental Sounds
5. Ambient Sounds

Important information should never be drowned out.

---

# Performance Rules

To optimize performance:

* Distant sounds fade naturally.
* Inactive sound sources stop processing.
* Repeated sounds reuse audio assets efficiently.

---

# Design Rules

* No excessive background music.
* No repetitive sound loops.
* Every interaction should have appropriate audio feedback.
* Silence should be used intentionally.
* Audio must always support immersion.

---

# Final Rule

Audio should make the world feel alive.

Every sound—whether a creaking floorboard, a distant thunderstorm, or a quiet voice recording—should deepen the player's connection to the story and reinforce the feeling of investigating a real place.
