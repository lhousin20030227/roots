MUSIC COMPOSITION
Purpose
Music Composition defines the standards for all original music in ROOTS, ensuring every piece serves emotional storytelling and supports the game's melancholic, introspective atmosphere.

Music Philosophy
Music in ROOTS is narrative support, not entertainment.
Compositions should enhance mood and emotion without drawing attention to themselves — the player should feel the music emotionally before consciously noticing it exists.

Musical Identity
ROOTS music is:

Minimal — sparse instrumentation, plenty of silence and space.
Acoustic — organic instruments favored over electronic (though subtle electronic elements acceptable).
Melancholic — emotional weight without despair.
Personal — intimate scale, small ensemble feel.
Non-intrusive — fades into the background at key story moments.


Instrumentation
Preferred instruments:

Piano (primary emotional anchor).
Strings (violin, cello — for depth and warmth).
Acoustic guitar (for accessible warmth).
Subtle woodwinds (sparingly — oboe, clarinet for color).
Minimal percussion (soft, brushed, not driving rhythm).

Avoid:

Electric guitar or rock-style percussion.
Synthesizers or heavy electronic processing.
Drums or bass driving rhythm aggressively.


Compositional Approach

Simple, memorable motifs (not complex classical structures).
Emphasis on atmosphere over technical complexity.
Repetition acceptable — themes anchor emotional continuity.
Dynamic range controlled — builds should be subtle, never bombastic.


Music Pacing & Duration

Loops are 2–4 minutes (supports exploration pacing without repetition fatigue).
Intro/outro transitions smooth (no jarring starts or stops).
Fade-in / fade-out preferred over hard stops.


Music by Context
Chapter-Specific Themes
Each chapter has a recognizable theme or motif.

Warm, familial tones in memory sequences.
Cooler, more uncertain tones in investigation/discovery scenes.
Resolved, bittersweet tones in conclusion scenes.

Themes subtly shift across chapters to reflect emotional progression.

Location Themes
Key locations may have associated ambient musical cues.
Examples:

Family home: warm, nostalgic piano motif.
Investigation locations: sparse, investigative tones.

Location themes repeat across visits, creating psychological familiarity.

Emotional Moment Cues
Major story beats may have specific musical underscoring.

Discovery of critical evidence: slight musical swell (brief, not overwhelming).
Emotional confrontation: supportive, non-manipulative accompaniment.
Resolution: bittersweet closure motif.

Music should enhance emotion without dictating how player should feel.

Silence & Absence
Moments with no music are intentional and powerful.
Used for:

Quiet exploration and thought.
Dialogue scenes (music recedes, lets voice be primary).
Moments of shock or realization (silence hits harder than sound).


Technical Specifications

Format: WAV or lossless audio (no compression artifacts).
Sample rate: 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz.
Bit depth: 16-bit or 24-bit.
Loops must be seamless (no clicks or pops at loop points).


Mixing & Mastering

Music mixed to sit behind dialogue and ambient sound in priority.
Dynamic range preserved — not heavily compressed.
No clipping or distortion artifacts.
Frequency balance: warm, not harsh or bright.


Rights & Licensing

Original compositions owned by project.
No temp music replaced late in production (ensures consistency).
All composers credited appropriately.


Forbidden Musical Elements
Do not include:

Aggressive, dissonant, or jarring compositions.
Pop, rock, electronic, or dance-style music.
Overly complex classical structures.
Leitmotifs that feel "anime-style" or theatrical.
Music that overwhelms dialogue or thought.


Final Rule
Music in ROOTS should feel like it comes from inside the player's emotional experience — supporting contemplation, memory, and discovery without ever announcing itself as "music" to be admired.
