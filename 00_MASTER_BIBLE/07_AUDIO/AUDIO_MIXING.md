AUDIO MIXING
Purpose
Audio Mixing defines the technical standards for balancing, processing, and delivering all audio elements (dialogue, music, ambience, SFX) into a cohesive, professional mix that serves story and immersion.

Mixing Philosophy
The mix should feel natural and transparent — no element should draw attention to itself, but all elements should work together seamlessly.
Dialogue is always the priority; music and ambience support without overwhelming.

Mix Hierarchy & Priority
Audio elements layered by importance:

Dialogue (highest priority) — always intelligible, never buried.
Music — supports emotion, recedes during important story moments.
Ambient Sound — establishes location/mood, stays subliminal.
SFX / Interactive Sounds — clear and present, but not competing with dialogue.


Dialogue Mix Standards

Dialogue peak level: -18 dB to -12 dB LUFS (loudness units).
Dialogue always intelligible at all playback levels (from quiet headphones to speakers).
Compression applied gently (maintains natural dynamic range and emotion).
EQ applied for clarity (subtle boost in intelligible frequencies, no harsh peaks).
No reverb that obscures intelligibility.


Music Mix Standards

Music level: -24 dB to -18 dB LUFS (significantly behind dialogue).
Music never overwhelms or competes with story moments.
During dialogue scenes: music recedes to barely-heard support.
During exploration or emotional beats: music rises appropriately.
Smooth fade-in / fade-out transitions between cues.


Ambient Sound Mix Standards

Ambience level: -30 dB to -24 dB LUFS (subtle, subliminal).
Ambience should be felt rather than consciously heard.
Ambience provides space and location context without distracting.
Smooth crossfade between location ambiences (no jarring switches).


SFX / Interactive Sound Mix Standards

SFX level: -20 dB to -12 dB LUFS (clear and present, but behind dialogue).
Footsteps, object interactions, etc. provide satisfying feedback without dominating.
SFX gain varies by type (louder impacts, softer ambient effects).
No SFX should startle or be unexpectedly loud.


Dynamic Range Management

Overall mix maintains dynamic range (not heavily compressed into brick-wall limiting).
Quiet moments preserved (silence and space are part of the mix).
Loud moments present but controlled (no clipping or distortion).
Compression applied gently across elements (transparent, not obvious).


Frequency Balance

Overall tone warm and grounded (not bright or harsh).
Low frequencies: present but not boomy (sub-bass minimized unless intentional).
Mid frequencies: clear and present (where intelligibility and warmth live).
High frequencies: present but not fatiguing (not over-brightened).


Stereo Imaging & Panning

Dialogue centered (always mono or center-panned for clarity).
Music and ambience use stereo width for space (not extreme left/right).
SFX panned based on source location (footsteps move with character movement).
No jarring hard-panned elements.


Mix Reference Standards

Mix checked on multiple playback systems:

Stereo headphones (intimate reference).
Studio monitors (professional reference).
Consumer speakers (real-world reference).


Mix translated well across devices (no surprises when played elsewhere).


Loudness Normalization

Mix normalized to industry standard loudness (-14 LUFS for streaming, -23 LUFS for game audio).
Peak levels: -1 dB to 0 dB (headroom for mastering/export).
No clipping or distortion artifacts.


Environmental Reverb & Spatialization

Dialogue: minimal reverb (clear, intimate).
Music: subtle reverb creating space (not echo-heavy).
Ambience: reverb reflects space type (indoor ambience has room reverb, outdoor is dry).
No artificial surround effects or extreme processing.


Mixing Workflow

Mix starts with dialogue as anchor (all other elements build around it).
Music mixed next (supporting without overwhelming dialogue).
Ambience and SFX added to complete soundscape.
Multiple listening passes at different volumes and on different systems.
Frequent breaks to avoid ear fatigue and gain objective perspective.


Mix Delivery & Export

Master mix exported as stereo WAV (48 kHz, 24-bit).
Stems exported separately if needed for localization (dialogue, music, ambience, SFX).
Mix checked for artifacts or corruption after export.
Metadata and cue points embedded in files (for engine integration).


Accessibility Considerations
Support:

Clear, intelligible dialogue (no inaudible whispers or unclear accents).
Optional dialogue subtitles (accommodates hearing-impaired players).
Balanced mix (no element so loud it's distorted or harsh).


Forbidden Mixing Practices
Do not include:

Dialogue buried under music or ambience.
Overly compressed, brick-wall mix (no dynamic range).
Excessive reverb or echo effects.
Uncontrolled peaks or clipping.
Inconsistent levels across dialogue or scenes.
Harsh, fatiguing high-frequency content.


Final Rule
A good mix is invisible — the player should hear an immersive, emotionally authentic soundscape that feels natural and supportive, never consciously aware of mixing choices or technical processing.
