SOUND DESIGN
Purpose
Sound Design defines the standards for all interactive sound effects, ambient audio, and environmental soundscapes in ROOTS, ensuring every sound feels authentic, purposeful, and supports immersion.

Sound Design Philosophy
Sound effects in ROOTS should feel like genuine sounds from the world — not exaggerated, not "game-like," not drawing attention to themselves.
Every sound must have a clear source and a clear reason for existing.

Sound Categories
Interactive Sound Effects
Sounds triggered by player interaction.
Examples:

Footsteps on different surfaces (wood, carpet, concrete).
Opening/closing doors, drawers, containers.
Picking up objects.
Using keys, pressing buttons.
Reading documents (page turns, rustling).

Interactive sounds must:

Provide immediate audio feedback confirming action.
Sound realistic and grounded.
Vary slightly to avoid repetitive monotony.


Environmental Ambience
Background soundscapes that establish location and mood.
Examples:

Room tone (subtle room resonance, air, silence).
Outdoor ambience (wind, birds, distant traffic).
Weather sounds (rain, wind in trees).
Urban ambience (distant city hum, occasional vehicles).

Ambience should be almost subliminal — present enough to establish space, subtle enough to not demand attention.

Atmospheric Effects
Subtle environmental sounds reinforcing mood.
Examples:

Creaking wood or settling building sounds (age, abandonment).
Ticking clocks (time passage).
Distant voices or activity (world feels inhabited).
Clock chimes or bells (time markers).

Atmospheric effects are rare, intentional, and emotionally purposeful.

UI & System Sounds
Sounds accompanying menus, notifications, and interface actions.
Examples:

Menu selection tone (subtle, musical).
Objective complete chime (brief, non-jarring).
Evidence added to board (soft confirmation sound).
Save/Load completion tone (gentle, reassuring).

UI sounds kept minimal and musical, never harsh or mechanical.

Sound Quality Standards

Clean, noise-free recordings (no hum, hiss, or artifacts).
Realistic frequency response (not overly processed or compressed).
Appropriate gain levels (not clipping, not requiring boost).
Natural reverb/space (not bone-dry, not overly cavernous).


Footstep Sound Rules

Varies by surface type (wood, carpet, concrete, grass).
Varies by shoe type and character weight (subtle differences).
Realistic cadence matching walking pace.
Not exaggerated or "heavy-booted" sounding.

Footsteps anchor the player's sense of movement and presence.

Object Interaction Sound Rules

Sound indicates material and mechanism (metal vs. wood, smooth vs. resistant).
Speed and intensity match animation (door opens at realistic speed with matching sound).
Subtle variation across instances (not identical every time).
No unrealistic "game-like" foley (coins, level-up chimes, etc.).


Ambience Layering
Environmental sounds layered to create full soundscape:

Base layer: room tone or outdoor ambience (constant, subliminal).
Mid layer: gentle movement or subtle activity (breeze, distant sounds).
Accent layer: occasional specific sounds (bird, distant voice).

No layer should dominate; together they create immersive space.

Dynamic Audio Rules
Sounds adjust to context:

Outdoor sounds quieter indoors (realistic muffling).
Sounds change with time of day (day ambience vs. night ambience).
Emotional context affects volume/presence (quieter during tense moments).


Technical Specifications

Format: WAV or lossless audio (no compression artifacts).
Sample rate: 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz.
Bit depth: 16-bit or 24-bit.
Mono or stereo as appropriate (directional effects use stereo).


Frequency Balance

Footsteps and impacts: balanced mid-range presence.
Ambient sounds: slightly reduced high frequencies (less fatiguing).
UI sounds: musical, warm tone (not harsh or metallic).

Overall frequency balance warm and grounded, never bright or harsh.

Forbidden Sound Elements
Do not include:

Overly loud or startling sound effects.
Arcade-style beeps or mechanical sounds.
Exaggerated foley (cartoon-style impact sounds).
Synthesized sounds without grounded context.
Doppler effects or overly processed audio.
Looping ambient sounds with obvious repetition.


Final Rule
Every sound in ROOTS should feel sourced from the world itself — the player should never consciously think "I'm hearing sound design," only feel the authenticity of the space they inhabit.
