AUDIO DIRECTION
Purpose
Audio Direction defines the unified sonic identity of ROOTS, ensuring every sound — music, ambience, dialogue, effects — reinforces the quiet, melancholic, emotionally weighted atmosphere of the game.

Audio Philosophy
Audio in ROOTS is not background noise — it is a storytelling tool equal to visuals.
The soundscape should feel intimate, personal, and slightly withdrawn — never bombastic, never distracting from thought and exploration.

Sonic Identity
ROOTS audio prioritizes:

Subtlety — effects and ambience fade into awareness rather than demand attention.
Emotional authenticity — sound reinforces mood without manipulation.
Realism — sounds feel sourced from the world, not synthetic.
Restraint — silence and space are as important as sound.


Audio Categories
Music
Composed to support emotional arc and pacing, never to overshadow story.
See MUSIC_COMPOSITION.md.

Dialogue
Voice acting, recordings, voiceovers, and audio logs.
See DIALOGUE_RECORDING.md.

Ambient Sound / Ambience
Background soundscapes and environmental audio.
Examples:

Room tone (subtle indoor ambient).
Natural ambience (wind, birds, water).
Urban ambience (distant traffic, city hum).

Ambience should be almost subliminal — present enough to anchor realism, subtle enough to not distract.

Sound Design / SFX
Interactive effects, object interactions, feedback sounds.
See SOUND_DESIGN.md.

System Audio
UI sounds, notification sounds, menu navigation.
Kept minimal and musical, never jarring.

Frequency & Tone

Low frequencies minimized except where narratively important (ominous moments, heavy emotional beats).
Mid frequencies carry clarity (especially dialogue).
High frequencies used sparingly to avoid listener fatigue.

Overall tone is warm, grounded, and occasionally melancholic.

Dynamic Range

No ear-fatiguing loudness (audio engineered to comfort, not shock).
Compression applied tastefully to maintain presence without harshness.
Quiet moments as important as loud ones (music/ambience contrast).


Mixing Philosophy
All audio mixed to work naturally together:

Dialogue always intelligible (priority priority).
Music supports without overwhelming dialogue or ambient sound.
SFX and ambience layer naturally without competing.

See AUDIO_MIXING.md for specific mixing standards.

Platform Considerations
Audio optimized for:

Stereo headphones (intimate, personal delivery).
Stereo speakers (clear separation and balance).
5.1 surround (optional, but not required).

No specific surround mixing; stereo mix adapts gracefully to surround setups.

Consistency Rules

Audio style, tone, and processing consistent across all categories.
No jarring tonal shifts between scenes or chapters.
Familiar audio signatures (character themes, location ambiences) reinforce world familiarity.


Forbidden Audio Elements
Do not include:

Loud, jarring start-up sounds or warning beeps.
Overly synthetic or "game-like" sound effects.
Aggressive or overwhelming music.
Dialogue that sounds robotic or poorly recorded.
Ambient noise that distracts rather than supports.


Final Rule
Every sound in ROOTS must serve the feeling of an intimate, quiet, emotionally real place — audio should make the player feel like they are there, inside the story, never aware they are listening to "audio design."
