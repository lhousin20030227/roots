DIALOGUE RECORDING
Purpose
Dialogue Recording defines the standards for capturing, processing, and implementing all voice acting and dialogue audio in ROOTS, ensuring authenticity, clarity, and emotional resonance.

Dialogue Philosophy
Voice acting in ROOTS should sound like real people speaking — natural pacing, authentic emotion, no theatrical exaggeration.
Dialogue must always be intelligible and emotionally truthful, never performed for effect.

Voice Acting Standards
Performance Direction

Natural, conversational delivery (not theatrical or melodramatic).
Emotional authenticity (actors should understand character motivation).
Appropriate pacing (people rarely rush their words; pauses matter).
Minimal exaggeration (subtle emotion reads better than broad performance).


Character Voice Consistency
Each character maintains consistent:

Vocal tone and pitch range.
Speech patterns and rhythm.
Emotional baseline and variation.

Actors should reference character profiles from 04_CHARACTERS/ for consistency across all dialogue sessions.

Dialogue Variation
Where dialogue repeats across multiple playthroughs (e.g., NPC greetings):

Record multiple takes and alternate between them.
Slight variation in delivery keeps repetition from feeling obvious.
Never use identical audio clip twice in succession.


Recording Technical Standards
Recording Environment

Quiet, treated recording space (minimal background noise).
Consistent microphone setup across all recording sessions.
Isolation booth or treated closet acceptable (even professional studios use small spaces).


Microphone & Equipment

Quality condenser microphone (Rode Procaster, Audio-Technica AT2020, or equivalent).
Pop filter to minimize plosives (p, b, t sounds).
XLR cable and audio interface (not USB).
Studio headphones for actor monitoring.


Recording Settings

Sample rate: 48 kHz (industry standard for game audio).
Bit depth: 24-bit.
Gain set to peak around -6 dB (leaving headroom, no clipping).
No compression or effects applied during recording (apply in post-production).


Recording Process

Multiple takes of each line (3–5 takes minimum).
Actor and director should discuss intention before recording.
Pauses and natural breaks captured (used in lip sync).
Bracketing: record slightly before and after dialogue (for editor flexibility).


Post-Production Standards
Audio Editing

Remove clicks, pops, and mouth sounds (breath before dialogue, dry mouth clicks).
Trim silence at beginning/end (but preserve natural pauses within dialogue).
Consistent gain across all dialogue (normalized to appropriate level).


Noise Reduction

Apply minimal, transparent noise reduction (preserves natural acoustic).
Never over-process (remove hum/hiss, but keep room tone).
Avoid artifacts or digital artifacts.


Equalization

Subtle EQ for clarity (slight boost in intelligible frequency range).
Warm tone preserved (not bright or harsh).
No extreme filtering or coloration.


Compression & Dynamics

Gentle compression for consistency (not heavy brick-wall limiting).
Preserves natural dynamic range of performance.
Maintains audible emotion and variation.


Dialogue Quality Check
All dialogue verified for:

Clarity and intelligibility.
Emotional authenticity.
Consistency with character voice.
No technical artifacts (pops, clicks, digital noise).
Appropriate gain level (sits well in mix with music/ambience).


Lip Sync Preparation

Audio exported with clear start point (silence trimmed, but timing noted).
Phoneme breakdown provided to animators (or automated via software).
Timing accuracy critical (animation lip sync must match frame-precisely).


Localization Considerations
If dialogue is translated to other languages:

Original English recorded first.
Translation maintains meaning and character voice.
Foreign language recording follows same technical standards.
Lip sync re-timed for translated audio (animations may need adjustment).


File Organization & Naming
All dialogue files named consistently per NAMING_STANDARD.md:
Example: DLG_CHARACTER_SCENE_LINE_001.wav

DLG_ = dialogue prefix.
CHARACTER = character name abbreviation.
SCENE = scene/chapter identifier.
LINE = sequential line number.
_001 = take number.


Rights & Licensing

All voice actors credited.
Recording rights clarified upfront (buyout vs. royalties).
Character voice ownership belongs to project.
No temp dialogue replaced late in production (ensures consistency).


Forbidden Dialogue Practices
Do not include:

Overly theatrical or "anime-style" voice acting.
Heavily accented performances that obscure intelligibility.
Robotic, monotone, or emotionless delivery.
Dialogue that sounds poorly recorded or processed.
Voice acting that contradicts character personality from 04_CHARACTERS/.


Final Rule
Dialogue in ROOTS should sound like real people having real conversations — authentic, emotionally true, and always in service of the story, never in service of impressive vocal performance.
