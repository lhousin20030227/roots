# WORLD

## Purpose

This document defines the game world of ROOTS. It establishes the environment, atmosphere, realism, and design principles that every location must follow.

---

# World Overview

ROOTS takes place in a small modern town.

The town is peaceful, quiet, and ordinary.

Nothing about the town should immediately appear mysterious.

The mystery comes from hidden truths within people's lives, not from the world itself.

---

# Tone

The world should feel:

* Calm
* Melancholic
* Believable
* Familiar
* Isolated
* Emotional

Players should feel like they are exploring real places where real people once lived.

---

# Scale

The game is **not** open world.

Instead, it consists of several carefully designed locations connected through the story.

Each location is compact, detailed, and meaningful.

Quality is always preferred over size.

---

# Accessible Locations

During Part 1, the player may visit locations such as:

* Adoptive Parents' House
* Biological Father's House
* Hospital
* City Hall
* Public Archive
* Cemetery

Additional locations may be introduced only if they support the narrative.

---

# Environmental Storytelling

Every location must communicate information through its environment.

Examples include:

* Furniture placement
* Lighting
* Weathering
* Family photographs
* Personal belongings
* Notes and documents
* Sound ambience

The environment should tell stories without relying on dialogue.

---

# Exploration

Exploration is always intentional.

Players are rewarded with:

* New evidence
* New documents
* New memories
* New objectives
* Better understanding of the story

Exploration should never feel like wandering without purpose.

---

# World Progression

The world expands as the investigation progresses.

Players unlock new locations only after obtaining sufficient evidence.

Progression is driven by knowledge, not by arbitrary game progression.

---

# World Design Principles

Every location must:

* Serve the story.
* Feel believable.
* Have a practical purpose.
* Contain meaningful details.
* Support exploration.
* Respect the project's realistic setting.

---

# Final Rule

The world exists to support the player's emotional journey.

Every street, room, object, and building should help tell the story of one family and the search for the truth.
