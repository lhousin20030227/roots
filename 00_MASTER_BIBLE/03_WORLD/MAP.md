# MAP

## Purpose

This document defines how the game world is connected. It is a logical progression map, not a geographical map.

---

# World Structure

The player starts in one central location.

As new evidence is discovered, additional locations become accessible.

The world expands through investigation.

---

---

# Unlock Rules

A new location may only become available when:

* A required clue is found.
* The previous objective is completed.
* The story logically supports the transition.

No location should unlock randomly.

---

# Fast Travel

Fast Travel is disabled at the beginning.

It becomes available only after the player has physically visited a location for the first time.

---

# Backtracking

Returning to previous locations is encouraged.

Older locations may contain:

* New documents.
* New dialogue.
* Newly unlocked rooms.
* Additional evidence.

Backtracking should always reward the player.

---

# Hidden Areas

Some areas remain inaccessible until specific evidence is obtained.

Examples:

* Locked rooms.
* Cabinets.
* Basements.
* Attics.
* Storage areas.

These areas must always have a believable reason for being locked.

---

# Future Expansion

The map is designed to support future chapters without redesigning the existing world.

Part 2 can introduce entirely new locations while keeping the progression system consistent.
