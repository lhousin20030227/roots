# ECONOMY

## Purpose

This document defines the economic rules of the world in ROOTS.

The economy exists only to support realism and environmental storytelling.

It is **not** a gameplay system.

---

# Economic Setting

The town has a stable but modest local economy.

Most people live ordinary middle-class lives.

Financial struggles exist, but they are personal rather than societal.

---

# Employment

Common professions include:

* Doctors
* Nurses
* Government Employees
* Teachers
* Mechanics
* Shop Owners
* Construction Workers
* Police Officers

NPC professions should always feel believable.

---

# Money

Money is **not** a gameplay mechanic.

The player cannot:

* Earn money
* Buy items
* Sell items
* Trade resources

The investigation never depends on currency.

---

# Property

Buildings are privately or publicly owned.

Examples:

* Family Houses
* Hospital
* City Hall
* Public Archive
* Cemetery

Ownership should always make sense within the story.

---

# Financial Background

The biological father's financial decline is an important part of the narrative.

His unemployment, addiction, and illness gradually destroyed his quality of life.

The adoptive family, while not wealthy, lived a stable and comfortable life.

---

# Gameplay Rule

The economy is part of the world's realism only.

There are:

* No shops
* No inventory prices
* No economy simulation
* No resource management
* No crafting materials

---

# Final Rule

Economics should support believable storytelling without becoming a gameplay feature. The player's progress is driven by knowledge and evidence—not by money.
