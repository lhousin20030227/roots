# CULTURE

## Purpose

This document defines the social and cultural atmosphere of the world in ROOTS.

The culture should feel familiar, realistic, and timeless.

It exists to support immersion—not to become the focus of the story.

---

# Community

The town is small enough that many people recognize each other.

Neighbors know local families.

People remember important events such as:

* Births
* Deaths
* Weddings
* Funerals
* Families moving away

---

# Family Values

Family is an important part of everyday life.

Parents generally try to protect their children.

This is why the adoption remained a secret.

The decision was made out of love rather than malice.

---

# Daily Life

Residents live ordinary lives.

Typical activities include:

* Going to work
* Attending school
* Shopping
* Visiting relatives
* Maintaining their homes

The player should feel that life continues even without them.

---

# Communication

People communicate naturally.

Dialogue should be:

* Respectful
* Believable
* Concise
* Emotionally authentic

Avoid exaggerated or theatrical conversations.

---

# Traditions

The world includes common real-life traditions such as:

* Family gatherings
* Funerals
* Birthdays
* Visiting cemeteries
* Keeping family photo albums

These traditions help strengthen emotional storytelling.

---

# Environment

Homes reflect the personalities of their owners.

Objects, decorations, photographs, and furniture should tell silent stories about the people who lived there.

---

# Storytelling Rule

Culture should be shown through the environment and character behavior rather than explained through exposition.

Players should understand the world by observing it.

---

# Final Rule

The culture of ROOTS must always feel genuine, grounded, and human. Every cultural detail should reinforce the game's central themes of family, memory, identity, and truth.
