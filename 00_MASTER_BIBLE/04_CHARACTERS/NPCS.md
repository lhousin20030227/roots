# NPCS

## Purpose

This document defines the design principles for all non-playable characters (NPCs) in ROOTS.

NPCs exist to make the world feel alive, believable, and emotionally authentic.

---

# NPC Philosophy

Every NPC should feel like a real person living their own life.

The player is only one small part of their world.

Each NPC has:

* A daily routine.
* A personal history.
* A profession.
* Relationships.
* Personal goals.
* Personal problems.

---

# NPC Categories

## Main NPCs

Characters who directly influence the main story.

Examples:

* Biological Father
* Neighbors
* Hospital Staff
* Archive Employee

---

## Secondary NPCs

Characters who support exploration and world building.

Examples:

* Shop Owner
* Teacher
* Mail Carrier
* Caretaker
* Cemetery Worker

---

## Background NPCs

Characters that exist to make the world feel alive.

Examples:

* Pedestrians
* Drivers
* Park Visitors
* Families
* Office Workers

These NPCs rarely interact with the player.

---

# Dialogue Rules

NPC dialogue should:

* Sound natural.
* Be concise.
* Match the NPC's personality.
* Reveal information gradually.

No NPC should explain the entire story.

---

# Knowledge Rules

Every NPC only knows what they could realistically know.

For example:

* A hospital employee knows medical procedures.
* A neighbor remembers local events.
* A government employee knows official records.

No NPC has impossible knowledge.

---

# Interaction Rules

NPCs should respond based on:

* The player's progress.
* Previously discovered evidence.
* Their relationship with the player.

Dialogue may change as the investigation advances.

---

# Behavior Rules

NPCs should:

* Walk.
* Sit.
* Work.
* Read.
* Talk.
* Drive.
* Rest.

Idle behavior should feel believable.

---

# Visual Diversity

NPCs should have varied:

* Ages
* Clothing
* Hairstyles
* Occupations
* Body types
* Personalities

Avoid repetitive appearances.

---

# AI Generation Rules

Every newly generated NPC must include:

* Full Name
* Age
* Occupation
* Personality
* Daily Routine
* Relationship to the Story
* Dialogue Style
* Gameplay Purpose

---

# Final Rule

No NPC should exist simply to fill space.

Every NPC must make the world feel more believable, even if the player only meets them once.
