# CHARACTER RULES

## Purpose

These rules ensure that every character in ROOTS remains believable, consistent, and emotionally authentic throughout the project.

---

# Core Principles

Every character must:

* Feel human.
* Have strengths and flaws.
* Have a personal history.
* Have clear motivations.
* Change naturally over time.

No character exists only to move the story forward.

---

# Realism

Characters must behave like real people.

Avoid:

* Unrealistic intelligence.
* Perfect memory.
* Instant trust.
* Dramatic speeches.
* Unrealistic emotional reactions.

---

# Dialogue Rules

Dialogue should be:

* Natural.
* Concise.
* Contextual.
* Emotionally honest.

People should rarely say exactly what they feel.

Subtext is preferred over direct exposition.

---

# Emotional Consistency

Every emotional reaction must have a believable cause.

Examples:

* Grief takes time.
* Trust develops gradually.
* Anger has consequences.
* Forgiveness is earned.

Avoid sudden personality changes.

---

# Knowledge Rules

A character only knows what they could realistically know.

Characters cannot reveal:

* Future events.
* Hidden information.
* Facts they never learned.

---

# Relationships

Relationships evolve naturally.

Trust, friendship, and reconciliation are built through repeated interactions—not a single conversation.

---

# Story Function

Every major character must contribute at least one of the following:

* Story progression.
* Emotional development.
* Gameplay.
* World building.
* Investigation.

If a character serves none of these purposes, they should be removed or redesigned.

---

# Design Restrictions

Characters must never exist as:

* Pure heroes.
* Pure villains.
* Walking tutorials.
* Exposition machines.
* Comic relief without purpose.

Everyone should feel like a real person with a life beyond the player.

---

# AI Rules

When generating new characters, AI Agents must always define:

* Full Name
* Age
* Occupation
* Background
* Personality
* Motivation
* Relationships
* Story Role
* Gameplay Role
* Visual Description

No character may contradict the MASTER BIBLE.

---

# Final Rule

Players should remember characters because they felt real—not because they delivered important information. Every character in ROOTS must leave a believable emotional impression.
