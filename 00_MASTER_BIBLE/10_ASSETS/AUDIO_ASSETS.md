# AUDIO ASSETS

## Purpose

This document defines the standards for all audio assets used in ROOTS.

Audio assets should support realism, storytelling, and immersion while remaining organized and optimized.

---

# Philosophy

Every sound should have a purpose.

Silence is preferable to unnecessary noise.

Audio should help the player feel present in the world.

---

# Audio Categories

## Ambient Sounds

Background environmental audio.

Examples:

* Wind
* Rain
* Birds
* Traffic
* Insects
* Ocean waves
* Air conditioning

Each location should have its own ambient identity.

---

## Sound Effects (SFX)

Short interactive sounds.

Examples:

* Door opening
* Footsteps
* Key turning
* Drawer sliding
* Light switch
* Paper handling
* Glass breaking

Every interaction should have appropriate feedback.

---

## Character Audio

Human-related sounds.

Examples:

* Dialogue
* Breathing
* Coughing
* Crying
* Laughing
* Footsteps
* Clothing movement

Character audio should match emotions and actions.

---

## Music

Music should be used sparingly.

Suitable moments include:

* Main menu
* Chapter introductions
* Emotional scenes
* Ending
* Credits

Exploration should rely primarily on ambient audio.

---

## Evidence Audio

Audio recordings collected during the investigation.

Examples:

* Cassette tapes
* Voicemail
* Voice recorder files
* Radio broadcasts

Players may replay them at any time.

---

# File Formats

Preferred formats:

* `.ogg` → General in-game audio
* `.wav` → Original high-quality recordings
* `.mp3` → Avoid unless necessary

---

# Naming Convention

Examples:

```text
door_open_01.ogg
footstep_wood_03.ogg
rain_heavy_loop.ogg
hospital_ambient_day.ogg
cassette_family_message_01.ogg
```

Use lowercase letters and underscores only.

---

# Optimization

* Compress audio appropriately.
* Remove unnecessary silence.
* Use looping only where needed.
* Reuse common sounds.
* Stream long music tracks when appropriate.

---

# Volume Standards

Priority order:

1. Dialogue
2. Evidence Audio
3. Important Sound Effects
4. Ambient Sounds
5. Music

Critical information should always remain clearly audible.

---

# Design Rules

* No copyrighted audio.
* No low-quality recordings.
* No clipping or distortion.
* No repetitive sounds without variation.
* Match audio to the environment and context.

---

# Final Rule

Every audio asset in ROOTS should strengthen immersion and emotion. Players should believe they are listening to a real world, where every sound has a natural source and meaningful purpose.
