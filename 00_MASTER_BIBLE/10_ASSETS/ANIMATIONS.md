# ANIMATIONS

## Purpose

This document defines the animation standards used throughout ROOTS.

Animations should enhance realism, communicate character intent, and provide responsive gameplay without unnecessary complexity.

---

# Philosophy

Every animation should have a purpose.

Movement should feel natural, subtle, and believable.

Avoid exaggerated or theatrical motions.

---

# Animation Categories

## Player Animations

Examples:

* Idle
* Walk
* Run
* Crouch (if implemented)
* Look Around
* Interact
* Pick Up
* Inspect Item

Player animations should always feel responsive.

---

## NPC Animations

Examples:

* Idle
* Walk
* Sit
* Stand
* Read
* Write
* Eat
* Drink
* Talk
* Wave
* Open Door

NPC animations should reflect personality and current activity.

---

## Object Animations

Examples:

* Doors opening
* Drawers sliding
* Cabinets opening
* Light switches
* Elevators
* Gates

Object movement should follow realistic physics and timing.

---

## Environmental Animations

Examples:

* Tree branches moving
* Curtains swaying
* Ceiling fans
* Rain effects
* Flowing water
* Flickering lights (when story-appropriate)

These animations make the world feel alive.

---

# Transition Rules

Animation transitions must be:

* Smooth
* Responsive
* Free from noticeable popping
* Consistent in speed

Blending should feel natural.

---

# Interaction Animations

Important interactions should include animation.

Examples:

* Picking up an object.
* Opening a drawer.
* Unlocking a door.
* Reading a document.
* Using a key.

Animations should never unnecessarily delay gameplay.

---

# Performance Rules

* Reuse animation clips whenever possible.
* Optimize bone counts.
* Limit unnecessary keyframes.
* Pause off-screen animations when appropriate.
* Use animation compression where possible.

---

# Realism Rules

Animations should respect:

* Human anatomy.
* Object weight.
* Natural timing.
* Environmental context.

Avoid robotic or exaggerated movement.

---

# Design Rules

* Every animation has a purpose.
* Keep movements believable.
* Prioritize responsiveness over spectacle.
* Maintain consistency across all characters and objects.

---

# Final Rule

Animations in ROOTS should make the world feel alive.

Players should feel that every person, object, and environment moves naturally, reinforcing immersion without distracting from the investigation.
