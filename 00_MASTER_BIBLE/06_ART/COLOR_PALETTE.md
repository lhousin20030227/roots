COLOR PALETTE
Purpose
The Color Palette defines the consistent color language used across ROOTS, ensuring every environment, character, and prop reinforces the same emotional tone.

Palette Philosophy
Colors in ROOTS are muted, naturalistic, and emotionally driven.
No location should rely on saturated or artificial color to stand out — mood comes from light, material, and restraint.

Primary Palette Categories
Neutral Base
Used for most architecture, furniture, and structural surfaces.

Warm greys
Soft beiges
Faded whites
Muted browns

These form the visual "skeleton" of every location.

Warm Accents
Used for comfort, memory, and emotional warmth.

Soft amber
Worn wood tones
Faded gold
Dusty orange

Applied to lamps, wood furniture, photographs, and nostalgic spaces.

Cool Accents
Used for distance, isolation, and emotional weight.

Muted blue-grey
Faded teal
Cold concrete tones
Pale slate

Applied to night scenes, abandoned spaces, and moments of difficult discovery.

Desaturated Greens
Used sparingly for nature and outdoor transitions.

Faded olive
Muted sage
Worn moss tones

Never bright or vibrant — nature feels quiet, not lush.

Color and Story
Color shifts subtly with emotional context:

Warm tones during comforting memories or family scenes.
Cooler tones during isolation, mystery, or unresolved tension.
Desaturation increases in emotionally heavier chapters.

These shifts must remain subtle — never an obvious "mood filter."

Material Color Rules

Wood: warm, slightly faded, never glossy.
Metal: dull, matte, slightly worn.
Fabric: soft, muted, naturally aged.
Walls: neutral base with slight warm or cool bias depending on room mood.


Lighting Interaction
Color palette must work in harmony with LIGHTING_SYSTEM.md:

Warm light sources enhance warm accents.
Cool/dim light enhances cool accents and neutrals.
No color should look "wrong" under the intended lighting of its scene.


Consistency Rules

Every asset must pull from the same defined palette ranges.
No pure black or pure white — always slightly muted/toned.
No neon, fluorescent, or highly saturated colors anywhere in the game.
Palette consistency checked during Art Review.


Forbidden Colors
Do not use:

Bright primary colors (pure red, blue, yellow) as dominant tones.
Neon or glowing colors without narrative justification.
High-contrast "game UI" colors in the environment.
Pure black shadows or pure white highlights.


Final Rule
Color in ROOTS should feel observed from real life, not designed for visual excitement.
Every hue must support mood, memory, and quiet realism — never call attention to itself.
