CHARACTER ART
Purpose
Character Art defines the visual standards for all human characters in ROOTS, ensuring consistency between geometry, texture, and the emotional realism the story requires.

Visual Approach
Characters use 3D Low Poly models with Pixel Art textures, matching the same visual language as the environment.
Characters should read as real people first — believable proportions and grounded design take priority over stylization.

Proportions

Realistic human proportions (no chibi, no exaggerated heads/eyes).
Consistent height scale across all characters relative to the world (doors, furniture).
Subtle individual variation (height, build) to distinguish family members naturally.


Geometry Rules

Low poly count, optimized for first-person proximity viewing.
Clean, simple silhouettes — no unnecessary geometric detail.
Faces are simplified but must support basic readable expressions.
Hands are simplified but must look correct during interactions (holding objects, opening doors).


Texture Rules

Pixel Art textures applied consistently with PIXEL_ART_GUIDE.md density.
Skin tones stay muted and natural, never overly saturated.
Clothing textures show subtle wear (creases, fading) appropriate to character and time period.
No glossy or shiny material shaders on skin or fabric.


Character Identity Through Art
Each character's visual design reflects their role and emotional context:

Adoptive Father / Mother — warm, worn clothing tones; lived-in appearance.
Biological Father / Mother — distinct subtle visual cues tied to their story role (era-appropriate clothing, more muted/distant tones where narratively relevant).
Older Brother — casual, grounded clothing consistent with his personality.
Player Character — neutral, relatable design; avoids overly distinctive features that could clash with player projection.

All visual storytelling must align with 04_CHARACTERS/ documents — no contradictions.

Facial Expressions
Supported baseline expressions:

Neutral
Warm / Smiling
Concerned
Sad
Surprised

Expressions stay subtle and grounded — no exaggerated cartoon expressions.

Clothing & Costume Rules

Clothing reflects character personality, role, and time period.
Limited outfit variations per character (no unnecessary wardrobe complexity).
Clothing wear (fading, wrinkles) reinforces realism and time passage.


Animation Compatibility
Character models must be rigged to support:

Walking / idle animations
Basic interaction animations (reaching, opening, holding)
Simple emotional gestures
Lip sync for dialogue (see ANIMATION.md)


Consistency Rules

Same poly budget across all main characters.
Same texture resolution across all main characters.
No character should visually contradict their established personality or background.


Forbidden Visual Elements
Do not include:

Hyper-realistic faces clashing with low poly bodies.
Exaggerated proportions or cartoon-style features.
Inconsistent scale between characters.
Modern or anachronistic clothing without story justification.


Final Rule
Every character in ROOTS must look like someone who could genuinely exist — their appearance should quietly reflect who they are and what they've lived through, never distract through style.
