ART DIRECTION
Purpose
The Art Direction defines the unified visual identity of ROOTS, ensuring every asset — environments, characters, props, and effects — feels like part of the same believable world.

Visual Identity
ROOTS combines two complementary visual languages:

3D Low Poly models for geometry (environments, characters, props).
Pixel Art textures applied to that geometry.

The result should feel handcrafted, intimate, and slightly nostalgic — never cartoonish or arcade-like.

Core Visual Goals

Realism through simplicity, not detail overload.
Emotional warmth balanced with melancholy.
A world that feels lived-in, aged, and personal.
Clarity: the player should always read shapes and spaces easily.


Low Poly Philosophy

Geometry stays simple and intentional — no unnecessary edge loops or detail.
Silhouettes must be clear and recognizable from gameplay distance.
Realism comes from proportion, scale, and lighting — not polygon count.
Every model respects real-world scale.


Pixel Art Texture Philosophy

Textures carry the detail that geometry doesn't.
Consistent pixel density across all assets (defined in PIXEL_ART_GUIDE.md).
No anti-aliasing or blurring on textures — pixels stay crisp.
Texture style stays grounded and realistic, not stylized/cute.


Color Philosophy

Muted, naturalistic palette (see COLOR_PALETTE.md).
Color reinforces emotional tone of each location and chapter.
Avoid saturated or "game-like" colors unless narratively justified.


Consistency Rules

Every asset must match the established poly budget and texture resolution.
No mixing of unrelated art styles (e.g., no high-poly realistic assets next to low poly ones).
Reused assets (doors, furniture, props) maintain identical style across all locations.


Mood & Atmosphere
The art direction supports:

Quiet, melancholic realism.
A sense of personal history in every space.
Environmental storytelling through wear, placement, and lighting.


Forbidden Visual Elements
Do not include:

Bright, saturated, cartoonish colors.
High-poly photorealistic assets.
Stylized/exaggerated proportions (no big heads, no chibi style).
Glowing or "gamey" visual effects without narrative purpose.
Inconsistent texture resolution between assets.


Final Rule
Every visual element in ROOTS must serve the feeling of a real, quiet, emotionally weighted place — never decoration for its own sake.
