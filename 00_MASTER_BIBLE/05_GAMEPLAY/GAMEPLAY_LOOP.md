# GAMEPLAY LOOP

## Purpose

This document defines the primary gameplay loop of ROOTS. Every feature, mechanic, puzzle, and objective must reinforce this loop.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

The player repeatedly follows this cycle:

```text
Receive Objective
        ↓
Explore
        ↓
Observe
        ↓
Interact
        ↓
Collect Evidence
        ↓
Analyze Evidence
        ↓
Solve Puzzle
        ↓
Discover New Truth
        ↓
Unlock New Objective
        ↓
Explore Again
```

This loop continues until the end of the game.

---

# Step 1 — Receive Objective

The player receives a logical objective.

Examples:

* Search the attic.
* Find the missing key.
* Visit the hospital.
* Speak with a neighbor.
* Locate adoption records.

Objectives should always feel natural.

---

# Step 2 — Explore

The player searches the environment.

Exploration rewards:

* Hidden evidence.
* Documents.
* New locations.
* Story details.

---

# Step 3 — Observe

The player carefully examines the environment.

Observation includes:

* Reading notes.
* Inspecting photographs.
* Looking for unusual objects.
* Listening to environmental sounds.

Nothing important should rely only on UI markers.

---

# Step 4 — Interact

The player interacts with meaningful objects.

Examples:

* Open drawers.
* Unlock doors.
* Pick up documents.
* Use keys.
* Activate recorders.

Every interaction must have a purpose.

---

# Step 5 — Collect Evidence

Important discoveries are automatically recorded.

Evidence becomes available inside the Evidence Board.

Categories include:

* Documents
* Photos
* Audio
* Objects
* Witness Testimony

---

# Step 6 — Analyze Evidence

The player reviews collected information.

Connections between clues become clearer.

The game assists organization but never solves the mystery automatically.

---

# Step 7 — Solve Puzzle

Using collected information, the player solves realistic puzzles.

Successful puzzles unlock:

* New evidence.
* New dialogue.
* New locations.
* Story progression.

---

# Step 8 — Discover New Truth

Every completed objective should reveal something meaningful.

Each answer creates new questions.

Curiosity should continuously drive the player forward.

---

# Step 9 — Progress

The player receives a new objective.

The investigation expands naturally.

The gameplay loop begins again.

---

# Loop Design Principles

Every loop should provide at least one of the following:

* New story information.
* Character development.
* Emotional impact.
* World building.
* Meaningful progression.

The player should never feel that time was wasted.

---

# Final Rule

The gameplay loop must remain simple, consistent, and rewarding.

The player's motivation is always the same:

**Discover the truth, one clue at a time.**
