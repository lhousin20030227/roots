# CORE MECHANICS

## Purpose

This document defines the core gameplay mechanics of ROOTS. Every mechanic must support exploration, investigation, and emotional storytelling.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

The player progresses by repeating a simple but meaningful cycle:

Explore → Observe → Interact → Collect Evidence → Solve Puzzle → Discover Truth → Unlock New Area

Every mechanic must contribute to this loop.

---

# Primary Mechanics

## Exploration

The player freely explores accessible locations.

Exploration rewards curiosity with new clues, documents, and story progression.

---

## Observation

Players carefully inspect their surroundings.

Important objects should naturally attract attention through environmental design rather than excessive UI.

---

## Interaction

The player can interact with believable objects such as:

* Doors
* Drawers
* Cabinets
* Keys
* Notes
* Books
* Photographs
* Computers
* Audio Recorders
* Light Switches

Every interaction must have a purpose.

---

## Evidence Collection

Important discoveries are automatically recorded.

Evidence includes:

* Documents
* Photographs
* Audio recordings
* Keys
* Official records
* Personal belongings

Evidence drives story progression.

---

## Puzzle Solving

Puzzles are grounded in reality.

Examples:

* Finding hidden keys.
* Matching documents.
* Unlocking storage areas.
* Reconstructing timelines.
* Connecting related evidence.

No abstract or fantasy puzzles.

---

## Dialogue

NPC conversations reveal information naturally.

Dialogue changes depending on:

* Current objectives.
* Previously discovered evidence.
* Relationship with the NPC.

---

## Investigation

The player connects information gathered from multiple sources.

The game never gives complete answers immediately.

Progress comes from logical deduction.

---

# Design Principles

Every mechanic must be:

* Simple.
* Intuitive.
* Realistic.
* Story-driven.
* Easy to learn.
* Difficult to master through observation.

---

# Forbidden Mechanics

Never include:

* Combat.
* Weapon systems.
* Character levels.
* Experience points.
* Skill trees.
* Crafting.
* Hunger or thirst.
* Survival mechanics.
* Magic.
* Supernatural abilities.
* Quick Time Events as primary gameplay.

---

# Final Rule

If a mechanic does not improve exploration, investigation, immersion, or storytelling, it should not be included in ROOTS.
