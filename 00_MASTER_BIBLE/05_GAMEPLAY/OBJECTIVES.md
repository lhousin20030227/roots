# OBJECTIVES

## Purpose

The Objective System guides the player through the investigation without removing the sense of discovery.

Objectives should encourage exploration, not dictate every action.

---

# Objective Philosophy

Objectives answer:

**"What should I investigate next?"**

They should never explain **how** to solve a puzzle.

The player must think, observe, and connect clues independently.

---

# Objective Types

## Main Objectives

Required to complete the story.

Examples:

* Organize the family house.
* Find the adoption records.
* Visit the hospital.
* Locate the biological father.

---

## Investigation Objectives

Require collecting evidence and connecting clues.

Examples:

* Search the attic.
* Inspect old family photographs.
* Read the father's diary.
* Compare two official documents.

---

## Puzzle Objectives

Unlocked when enough evidence has been collected.

Examples:

* Unlock the hidden cabinet.
* Restore the damaged photograph.
* Find the missing key.
* Open the archive storage room.

---

## Optional Objectives

Provide additional lore and character development.

Examples:

* Read every family letter.
* Visit all graves in the cemetery.
* Find every childhood photograph.
* Learn the complete family history.

These objectives are optional but rewarding.

---

# Objective States

Every objective has one of the following states:

* Locked
* Available
* In Progress
* Completed
* Failed (only in rare scripted situations)

Objectives should update automatically as the investigation progresses.

---

# Objective Tracking

The Journal displays:

* Current Objective
* Completed Objectives
* Related Evidence
* Related Locations
* Related Characters

The Journal never provides puzzle solutions.

---

# Objective Design Rules

Every objective must:

* Have a clear purpose.
* Be logically connected to the story.
* Lead to meaningful discoveries.
* Respect the player's intelligence.

Avoid repetitive tasks.

---

# Progression Rules

Completing objectives may unlock:

* New locations.
* New dialogue.
* New evidence.
* New puzzles.
* New story chapters.

Progression is driven by investigation, not by arbitrary checkpoints.

---

# Final Rule

Objectives should guide the player toward the truth while preserving curiosity, freedom, and the satisfaction of solving the mystery through their own observations.
