# EVIDENCE SYSTEM

## Purpose

The Evidence System is the foundation of investigation in ROOTS.

Players do not level up through combat—they progress by discovering, understanding, and connecting evidence.

---

# Definition

Evidence is any piece of information that helps reveal the truth.

Evidence may be physical, digital, verbal, or environmental.

Every piece of evidence must have a believable origin.

---

# Evidence Categories

## Documents

Examples:

* Birth Certificate
* Adoption Papers
* Medical Records
* Death Certificate
* Letters
* Official Reports

---

## Photographs

Examples:

* Family Photos
* Childhood Pictures
* Old Albums
* Framed Photos

Photographs often reveal hidden relationships.

---

## Audio

Examples:

* Voice Recordings
* Answering Machine Messages
* Cassette Tapes

---

## Objects

Examples:

* Keys
* Jewelry
* Personal Items
* Military Medal
* Diary

Objects often unlock new questions.

---

## Witness Testimony

Information obtained from NPC conversations.

Dialogue becomes evidence only when it contributes to the investigation.

---

## Environmental Evidence

Examples:

* Blood stains (if story relevant)
* Empty rooms
* Rearranged furniture
* Damaged objects
* Hidden compartments

The environment itself can reveal information.

---

# Evidence Quality

Evidence is divided into three levels.

## Minor Evidence

Adds background information.

Optional.

---

## Important Evidence

Unlocks new dialogue or objectives.

Required for progression.

---

## Critical Evidence

Directly advances the main story.

Cannot be missed.

---

# Evidence Board

All collected evidence is automatically organized.

Players can review:

* Documents
* Photos
* Audio
* Objects
* Character Notes
* Location Notes

The board helps players connect clues without solving the mystery for them.

---

# Investigation Rules

Evidence should:

* Lead naturally to another clue.
* Never appear randomly.
* Always have a logical location.
* Reward exploration.

Every discovery should answer one question while creating another.

---

# Progression

Story progression is controlled by evidence.

New locations, dialogue, and objectives unlock only after the required evidence has been obtained.

---

# Design Rules

* No random collectibles.
* No meaningless notes.
* No duplicate evidence.
* Every evidence item must have narrative value.
* Every critical clue must be obtainable through gameplay.

---

# Final Rule

Evidence is the player's greatest tool.

The truth is never given—it is earned through careful observation, logical thinking, and meaningful investigation.
