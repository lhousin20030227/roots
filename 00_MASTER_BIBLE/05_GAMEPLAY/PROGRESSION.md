# PROGRESSION

## Purpose

The Progression System controls how the player advances through ROOTS.

Progress is based on knowledge, evidence, and understanding—not experience points or character upgrades.

---

# Progression Philosophy

The player progresses by answering questions.

Each discovery unlocks new opportunities to investigate further.

The game rewards curiosity and logical thinking.

---

# Core Progression Loop

```text
Objective
    ↓
Explore
    ↓
Collect Evidence
    ↓
Analyze Information
    ↓
Solve Puzzle
    ↓
Unlock New Story Event
    ↓
Unlock New Location
    ↓
Receive New Objective
```

---

# Progression Gates

The player advances only after meeting logical conditions.

Examples:

* Finding a required document.
* Completing an important conversation.
* Solving a puzzle.
* Obtaining a specific key.
* Connecting multiple pieces of evidence.

---

# Story Progression

The main story is divided into chapters.

Each completed chapter unlocks:

* New objectives.
* New dialogue.
* New evidence.
* New locations.
* New gameplay opportunities.

---

# Exploration Progression

Exploration expands naturally.

Previously inaccessible areas become available after obtaining the correct information or items.

Examples:

* Locked rooms.
* Hidden storage.
* Government archives.
* Medical records.

---

# Character Progression

Relationships evolve over time.

NPC dialogue changes according to:

* Story progress.
* Player knowledge.
* Previous conversations.
* Collected evidence.

Trust is earned gradually.

---

# Evidence Progression

Evidence is the primary progression resource.

Every important discovery should:

* Confirm previous theories.
* Introduce new mysteries.
* Unlock additional investigation paths.

---

# Objective Progression

Objectives update automatically.

The player is never forced to complete optional content before continuing the main story.

Optional objectives enrich the narrative without blocking progress.

---

# Failure Philosophy

ROOTS avoids traditional failure states.

The player cannot permanently miss critical evidence.

Mistakes may delay progress but should never make the game unwinnable.

---

# Completion

A chapter is complete when:

* All required objectives are finished.
* Critical evidence has been collected.
* The next story event has been unlocked.

---

# Design Rules

* No XP.
* No level system.
* No grinding.
* No artificial progression barriers.
* No repetitive tasks.

Progress must always feel earned through investigation.

---

# Final Rule

Progress in ROOTS is measured by understanding.

The player does not become stronger—they become closer to the truth.
