# INVENTORY

## Purpose

The Inventory stores every meaningful item collected during the game.

It is designed to support exploration, investigation, and puzzle solving—not resource management.

---

# Philosophy

The inventory should remain:

* Clean
* Organized
* Easy to navigate
* Realistic

Players should never carry unnecessary or useless items.

---

# Inventory Categories

## Evidence

Items directly related to the investigation.

Examples:

* Adoption Papers
* Birth Certificate
* Medical Records
* Letters
* Diaries
* Photographs

---

## Keys

Items that unlock access to new areas.

Examples:

* House Keys
* Cabinet Keys
* Storage Keys
* Office Keys

Keys remain in the inventory after use unless the story requires otherwise.

---

## Personal Items

Objects with emotional or narrative value.

Examples:

* Wedding Ring
* Pocket Watch
* Family Album
* Military Medal
* Childhood Toy

These items enrich the story and may unlock dialogue or memories.

---

## Puzzle Items

Items required to solve specific puzzles.

Examples:

* Missing Photograph
* Broken Device
* Old Map
* Password Note

Once their purpose is fulfilled, they may remain as collectibles or become archived.

---

# Item Information

Every inventory item contains:

* Name
* Description
* Category
* Date Found
* Location Found
* Story Importance
* Related Characters
* Related Objectives

---

# Inspection

Players can inspect every important item.

Inspection may reveal:

* Hidden writing
* Signatures
* Dates
* Damage
* Marks
* Additional clues

Careful observation is rewarded.

---

# Inventory Rules

* Unlimited capacity.
* No weight system.
* No item durability.
* No item crafting.
* No duplicate key items.
* No useless collectibles.

Every item must have a purpose.

---

# Automatic Organization

Items are automatically sorted by category.

The player may also sort by:

* Recently Collected
* Story Importance
* Location
* Alphabetical Order

---

# Story Integration

Some inventory items unlock:

* New dialogue
* New objectives
* New locations
* Puzzle solutions
* Character reactions

NPCs may respond differently depending on the items the player possesses.

---

# Final Rule

Every item in the inventory must answer one question, create a new question, or help the player move closer to discovering the truth. If an item serves none of these purposes, it should not exist in ROOTS.
