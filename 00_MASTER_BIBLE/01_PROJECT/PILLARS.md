# PROJECT PILLARS

Version: 1.1
Status: Draft
Author: ROOTS Project Documentation
Last Updated: 2026-07-01

## Purpose

The Project Pillars define the non-negotiable foundations of ROOTS. Every gameplay system, story event, level, puzzle, asset, and future decision must support at least one of these pillars.

---

# Pillar 1 — Exploration

The player learns by exploring. Every room, object, document, and environment should reward curiosity, and exploration should always feel meaningful.

---

# Pillar 2 — Investigation

The player is not told the truth; the player discovers it. Progress comes from observation, connecting clues, and understanding evidence.

---

# Pillar 3 — Emotional Storytelling

The emotional impact comes from believable people and realistic situations. Characters should feel human, and drama should emerge naturally from the story.

---

# Pillar 4 — Environmental Storytelling

The world itself tells the story. Players should understand events through furniture, photos, documents, lighting, sound, object placement, and building condition. The environment is a storyteller.

---

# Pillar 5 — Meaningful Interaction

Every interaction must have a purpose. Examples include reading a document, opening a drawer, inspecting a photo, listening to an audio recording, or unlocking a door. Meaningless interactions should be avoided.

---

# Pillar 6 — Logical Progression

Progress is earned through logic. Players advance by finding evidence, solving puzzles, unlocking new locations, and understanding the story. Never through random events.

---

# Pillar 7 — Immersion

Nothing should break immersion. Unnecessary UI, artificial tutorials, unrealistic mechanics, or excessive notifications should be avoided. The player should feel physically present inside the world.

---

# Pillar 8 — Simplicity

Simple systems executed well are better than many unfinished systems. Every feature must justify its existence, and if it does not improve the experience, it should not be added.

---

# Final Principle

Whenever a new feature or idea is proposed, ask:

1. Does it improve exploration?
2. Does it strengthen investigation?
3. Does it support the story?
4. Does it increase immersion?
5. Is it worth the development time?

If most answers are no, the feature should be rejected.
