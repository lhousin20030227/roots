# WORLD RULES

## Purpose

These rules define how the world of ROOTS operates. Every location, character, event, dialogue, puzzle, and future addition must follow these rules.

---

# Reality

The world is completely realistic.

Nothing supernatural exists.

There are no:

* Ghosts
* Demons
* Monsters
* Magic
* Curses
* Miracles
* Unexplained paranormal events

Everything has a logical explanation.

---

# Technology

The game takes place in a modern setting.

Technology is ordinary and believable.

Examples:

* Smartphones
* Computers
* Cars
* CCTV
* Radios
* Medical records
* Government databases

No futuristic technology is allowed.

---

# Society

People behave like ordinary citizens.

Characters have:

* Jobs
* Families
* Daily routines
* Personal problems

NPCs should never exist only to deliver information.

---

# Locations

Every location must have a believable purpose.

Examples:

* House
* Hospital
* City Hall
* Archive
* Cemetery
* Biological Father's House

No location exists only because it looks interesting.

---

# Documents

Every document must have:

* A creator
* A date
* A reason to exist
* A believable location

Documents cannot exist only to reveal story information.

---

# Evidence

Every clue must follow cause and effect.

Evidence must never appear randomly.

The player earns information through investigation.

---

# Puzzles

Every puzzle must be realistic.

Examples:

* Finding missing documents
* Matching photographs
* Unlocking cabinets with discovered keys
* Organizing records
* Repairing simple devices

Avoid abstract or game-like puzzles.

---

# Characters

Every character has:

* A personal history
* Motivations
* Relationships
* Strengths
* Weaknesses

No character should exist only as an exposition tool.

---

# Player Knowledge

The player only knows what has been discovered.

Never reveal future information before the required evidence has been found.

---

# Consistency Rule

If any new feature, dialogue, location, document, puzzle, or gameplay mechanic contradicts these rules, it must be rejected or the MASTER BIBLE must be updated before implementation.
