# STORY

## High Concept

ROOTS is a realistic first-person narrative mystery game about identity, family, loss, and truth.

The player returns to the house of the adoptive parents after their death, expecting only to organize their belongings and decide the future of the house.

Instead, a series of small inconsistencies slowly lead to a hidden truth that changes the player's entire understanding of their life.

The mystery begins long before the player realizes they were adopted.

The player investigates because something feels wrong, not because they already know what they are searching for.

---

# Core Theme

Identity.

The central question of the game is:

**"Who am I?"**

Everything in the story exists to support this theme.

---

# Narrative Style

The player discovers the story naturally.

There are no long cinematic sequences explaining events.

Information is obtained through:

* Exploration
* Documents
* Letters
* Photographs
* Audio recordings
* Conversations
* Environmental storytelling

---

# Story Structure

The story is divided into three major acts.

## Act I — Doubt

The player returns home.

The objective is simple:

Organize the house.

Small discoveries create unanswered questions.

Nothing directly reveals the adoption.

The player only becomes suspicious.

---

## Act II — Truth

Evidence slowly connects together.

The player discovers hidden documents.

Government records.

Hospital files.

Family photographs.

Eventually the player realizes that every clue points to one conclusion:

They were adopted.

The objective changes completely.

Now the player wants to know the truth.

---

## Act III — Acceptance

The investigation leads to the biological father.

The player learns about his past.

Attempts to help him.

Before rebuilding the relationship, the father dies.

Before his death he reveals one final truth:

The player has an older brother.

After the funeral the player returns home.

A final photograph becomes the bridge to Part 2.

---

# Ending

The player stands outside the old house.

Holding the family photograph.

The future is uncertain.

One question remains unanswered.

Where is the older brother?

Fade to black.

End of Part 1.

---

# Story Philosophy

The game is never about saving the world.

It is about understanding one life.

Every discovery should feel personal.

Every clue should matter.

Every answer should create a new question until the final scene.
